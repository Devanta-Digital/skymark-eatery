# Skymark Eatery — creative & CRO diagnosis (no mercy)

## What the site currently does well (keep)

- **Operational clarity:** Phone, hours, address, and catering inquiry path are always reachable; weekday utility is credible.
- **Local anchors:** Mississauga, Skymark Ave, Pearson-adjacent language is present and should stay explicit for SEO and trust.
- **Menu truth:** Static menu + live items mapping is honest for ordering; do not dilute operational accuracy for aesthetics.
- **Catering depth:** Buffet packages, trays, and dietary notes are a real commercial surface — the money page has substance.

## Where it still feels templated or “safe”

1. **Mid-tone fatigue:** Too many sections sit in the same warm-beige band without enough **reset** into deep surfaces or crisp white breaks. Premium hospitality almost always alternates **light → deep → light** with intent.
2. **Hero sameness:** Full-bleed dark hero is correct, but without a **distinctive compositional signature** (asymmetry, editorial crop, or typographic moment), it reads like “good restaurant template.”
3. **Equal-weight UI:** Cards and shells repeat similar border/shadow language; nothing reads as the **one hero gesture** (e.g. a single terracotta rail, oversized numeral, or editorial pull-quote system).
4. **Takeout vs catering hierarchy:** Both are important, but **catering is revenue-critical** — the homepage must visually subordinate “daily lunch” to “we run serious office catering” without hiding takeout.
5. **Menu page = database vibe:** Rows are clear but scanning is fatiguing; **featured / house** picks need a curated band above the scroll wall.
6. **Contact = utility slab:** Functional but emotionally cold; needs **framing** (image, typographic hierarchy, scannable grid) without adding friction.
7. **Imagery risk:** Any photo that reads as **stock**, **busy**, or shows **third-party marks** undermines premium. Instagram-sourced real kitchen food is on-brand if **graded consistently** (warm shadow, tighter crops, no logo clutter).

## Image / art direction callouts

- **Hero pasta / trays:** Strong if crop keeps **text-safe negative space** and warm shadow grade is consistent.
- **Line / counter shots:** Prefer **tighter crops** and consistent warmth; avoid wide empty dining room frames that feel generic.
- **Rule:** No delivery-marketplace logos or branded cups in frame. If present, crop or replace.

## SEO / content structure (diagnosis only)

- Titles are strong but **page differentiation** can tighten: menu page should scream “weekday takeout” vs catering page “office catering Mississauga.”
- Internal linking: ensure **home → catering**, **menu → catering**, **catering → menu**, **contact → catering** are visually obvious (not only footer).

## Single-line verdict

Skymark is **credible and useful** but still **one pass away** from feeling like a **signature Mississauga hospitality brand** with a confident catering story.
